Conference Venue

The 2026 State of the Map conference sessions will be held on the campus of Université Gustave Eiffel in Cité Descartes, Champs-sur-Marne a suburb in the East of Paris.

The whole venue is wheelchair accessible. There are large open areas inside and outside of the venue, and features a cafeteria and restaurant. In the immediate vicinity one can find many amenities like shops, a pharmacy, etc. Head over to Panoramax to get an impression of the venue yourself.

© cquest, CC-BY-SA 4.0

The venue is located near the RER A local train line, connecting it to the centre of Paris as well as Disneyland Paris. From the station Noisy-Champs it is a short walk of approximately 600 metres (about 8 minutes) to the converence venue.

Getting to Paris

Railways

Paris is well served by train services. There are many direct connections to cities in France, as well as its neighbouring countries. Paris does further have an extensive public transport network which means getting to and around Paris is relatively straight forward.

Air Travel

Also by plane, Paris is quite easy to get to: There are great connections to Europe and the world from two major international airports: Roissy CDG and Orly ORY. There are also some low-cost options to airports in and around Paris.

Road

It is of course also possible to reach Paris by car or bus.

Visa

France is part of the Schengen area, so getting to it is open to all Europeans without any visa. Foreigners can find more information about the visa process and related details on france-visas.gouv.fr.
